A week of endings and formal recognition: the last survivor of the 2010s Shonen Jump mega-hit era closed out its run today, a two-year wait for a major manga's anime announcement finally ended, and two foundational figures in anime and manga history received formal honors from the Japanese government. Idol culture said a proper farewell, and the internet gathered in Makuhari once more.

## Black Clover Ends Today at Chapter 392 After Eleven Years

Yuki Tabata's *Black Clover* [reached its final chapter today](https://www.cbr.com/black-clover-final-chapter-2026-release/), publishing simultaneously in the Spring 2026 issue of Jump GIGA with chapters 390, 391, and 392. The series ran for eleven years — debuting in 2015, moving from Weekly Shonen Jump to quarterly Jump GIGA in 2023 as it wound down — ending with Asta crowned as the new Wizard King after the decisive battle against Yuno that the series had been building toward since its first chapter. *Black Clover* never matched the cultural footprint of its contemporaries *My Hero Academia* or *Demon Slayer*, but it finished on its own terms at volume 38 with a full epilogue rather than an abrupt cut, which is a better outcome than many series in its position get. The ongoing anime Season 2 is still in production on Crunchyroll, meaning the animated version will be catching up to a completed story — something that should benefit pacing in the back half of what remains to adapt.

## Kagurabachi Gets Its Anime — Studio Cypic, Spring 2027

On April 27, during a Jump Press livestream, [a TV anime adaptation of Takeru Hokazono's *Kagurabachi* was announced](https://www.crunchyroll.com/news/latest/2026/4/27/kagurabachi-tv-anime-announced-april-2027-premiere-trailer-visual) for an April 2027 premiere, with a [press release confirming the core production details](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/kagurabachi-set-for-tv-anime-adaptation-by-cypic-in-april-2027-302754426.html).

![Kagurabachi anime teaser visual — official trailer thumbnail (©Takeru Hokazono/SHUEISHA, ©Takeru,Project Kagurabachi)](https://i.ytimg.com/vi/pC39732i1bA/hqdefault.jpg)

The series will be animated by studio Cypic, directed by Tetsuya Takeuchi, with Keigo Sasaki on character designs and Taihi Kimura cast as protagonist Chihiro Rokuhira. The manga launched in Shonen Jump in 2023 and generated unusually intense early discussion given how quickly it appeared on top-10 lists — an anime announcement was never really a question of if, only when. Takeuchi's background is primarily in key animation on *Naruto*-adjacent projects, not in directing full series, which is the first thing worth watching as production news fills in. A world tour event showing the first 20 minutes of episode 1 is confirmed for summer 2026, ahead of the April 2027 broadcast.

## Tomino and Satonaka Receive Order of the Rising Sun

On April 29, the Japanese government [announced its Spring 2026 Conferment of Decorations](https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2026-04-29/gundam-creator-yoshiyuki-tomino-manga-creator-machiko-satonaka-inducted-into-order-of-the-rising-sun/.236913), inducting *Mobile Suit Gundam* creator Yoshiyuki Tomino, age 84, and shōjo manga pioneer Machiko Satonaka into the Order of the Rising Sun, Gold Rays with Neck Ribbon class. Tomino created and directed the original *Mobile Suit Gundam* in 1979; the franchise has generated over ¥800 billion in merchandise revenue and is arguably the most commercially durable intellectual property in anime history. Satonaka debuted in 1964 at age 16 with *Pia no Shōzō*, created a body of work including *Tenjō no Niji* and *Aries no Otome-tachi*, and has spent decades as a public advocate for recognizing manga as a legitimate art form — which makes her inclusion in the same honors cycle as Tomino pointed. State recognition of manga and anime figures remains relatively rare in Japan; the fact that both a mecha director and a shōjo pioneer are being honored in the same spring cycle reflects how broadly the medium's cultural standing has shifted. Tomino's direction across *Zeta Gundam*, *Victory Gundam*, and *Turn A Gundam* produced three of the most formally serious series in mecha anime — each critical of the franchise that funded them. The presentation ceremony is May 12. The [ANN encyclopedia entry on Tomino](https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/encyclopedia/person.php?id=177) is a reasonable starting point for tracing the full scope of his output.

## Mukaichi Mion's Final AKB48 Theater Performance

Mion Mukaichi performed her graduation theater stage at AKB48 Theater on April 30, [concluding a twelve-year career with the group](https://akb48.fandom.com/wiki/Mukaichi_Mion). She had announced her graduation in December 2025, held her graduation concert at Yoyogi National Gymnasium on April 3 titled *Watashi no Yume wa, AKB48* ("My Dream is AKB48"), and the [April 30 theater stage](https://www.nogizakatv.com/%E3%80%90%E5%85%AC%E6%BC%94%E9%85%8D%E4%BF%A1%E3%80%91akb48-260430-%E5%90%91%E4%BA%95%E5%9C%B0%E7%BE%8E%E9%9F%B3-%E5%8D%92%E6%A5%AD%E5%85%AC%E6%BC%94/) was her farewell to the theater itself — the institution that defines the AKB48 model more than any arena show. Mukaichi joined AKB48 in 2012, became the group's third General Manager in 2019, and stepped down from that role in 2023. In her graduation statement, she said: "AKB48 was my entire youth, my everything." Her departure coincides with AKB48's twentieth-anniversary period, which gives it added weight; she's leaving at an era-turning moment rather than in decline. Who fills the organizational and symbolic space she's vacated is the question to follow.

## Niconico Chokaigi 2026 Draws 138,228 Attendees; BokaColle Returns in August

Niconico Chokaigi 2026 [was held April 25–26 at Makuhari Messe](https://www.frontlinejp.net/2026/04/27/niconico-chokaigi-2026-event-photo-report/), drawing 138,228 attendees across both days — the event's strongest attendance figure since its pre-pandemic peak. The annual gathering functions as a physical reconstruction of Nico Nico Douga's internet culture, with simultaneous booths for VTubers, Vocaloid, doujinshi, itasha, cosplay, e-sports, gunpla, and more left-field presences including the Japan Self-Defense Forces, which returned to the event floor after a three-year absence with an exhibition of high-mobility vehicles and the opportunity to try on uniforms. The VOCALOID Collection (BokaColle) was announced during the event for its 12th edition, scheduled for August 2026 — the spin-off online event focused specifically on Vocaloid culture. M3-2026, the independent music creator market, ran concurrently on April 27 at Tokyo Ryutsu Center. Chokaigi has operated as a reliable barometer of where internet fan culture concentrates in any given year; the VTuber and Vocaloid communities have now consistently been the highest-density presences for several editions running.

## No Game No Life Returns After Three Years with Volume 13

Yū Kamiya's *No Game No Life* [light novel series returned on April 24](https://animecorner.me/no-game-no-life-returns-with-volume-13-in-april-first-new-release-in-over-3-years/) with Volume 13 — the first new volume since February 2023 — titled *The Gamer Siblings Seem to Defeat (Save) the Demon King*, continuing the Demonia arc and the assault on the Devil's Tower. The hiatus had no public explanation, which fans filled in extensively given Kamiya's notoriously volatile social media history over the years. The series still has no confirmed anime sequel despite the fandom remaining active; a second season remains one of the industry's most-requested and least-progressed projects. Whether Volume 13 is the start of a more regular cadence or another single-volume emergence before another gap is the only question that matters right now.

## Rascal Does Not Dream of a Dear Friend Reveals Character Visuals on a Weekly Countdown

The *Rascal Does Not Dream of a Dear Friend* film — the fourth and final entry in the *Bunny Girl Senpai* anime series, opening October 16, 2026 — has been releasing memorial character visuals every Sunday since mid-April, with [Mai Sakurajima's visual on April 19](https://www.crunchyroll.com/news/latest/2026/4/19/rascal-does-not-dream-of-a-dear-friend-anime-film-mai-sakurajima-visual) and [Kaede Azusagawa's on April 26](https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2026-04-26/rascal-does-not-dream-of-a-dear-friend-film-unveils-mai-sakurajima-kaede-azusagawa-visuals/.236811).

![Rascal Does Not Dream of a Dear Friend — Mai Sakurajima memorial visual (©Hajime Kamoshida/ASCII Media Works/Aniplex)](https://cdn.kurenai.com/photo/2/0/lW9jFMOsjqE/full.jpg)

CloverWorks is producing under returning director Sōichi Masui, with fox capture plan composing. The film adapts *Volume 15*, the final novel in the series. The decision to give Kaede equal visual prominence in the campaign alongside Mai is a deliberate signal about where the story's emotional weight falls in this conclusion; the Kaede arc remains the emotional center of the earlier TV series for most of the fanbase. The film's title, *Dear Friend*, is the title of the final light novel — the production is not being coy about what kind of story this is.
