A week split between endings and new territory: KyoAni's flagship brass-band series hit theaters today, Black Clover locked in its final publication date after eleven years, and Key dropped their first fully original VN since 2020. Elsewhere, Ghibli announced a park-exclusive short, two light-novel adaptations got greenlit, and the otome VN genre made its first PC move in nearly a decade.

![Night in the Valley of Witches — Studio Ghibli's first Ghibli Park-exclusive short film](https://comicbook.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/4/2026/04/Night-in-the-Valley-of-the-Witches.jpeg?w=724)

## Studio Ghibli's First Ghibli Park-Exclusive Short Film Debuts July 8

Studio Ghibli [announced on April 20](https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2026-04-20/studio-ghibli-animates-majo-no-tani-no-yoru-short-for-ghibli-park/.236613) that *Night in the Valley of Witches* (*Majo no Tani no Yoru*), their first animated short made specifically for Ghibli Park, will screen at the park's Cinema Orion beginning July 8. The film is co-directed by Goro Miyazaki and Akihiko Yamashita — a key animator whose credits run from *Spirited Away* through *The Wind Rises*. Only 150 tickets will be available for the premiere screening, with public sales opening May 10. This is the clearest signal yet that Ghibli Park intends to operate as a destination for original content rather than just a monument to existing films — a model borrowed from Disney Parks, where exclusive short films have been used to make a physical location worth repeated visits. Whether future Ghibli shorts follow this park-exclusive model or eventually surface on streaming is the question to watch as the park matures.

## Sound! Euphonium: The Final Movie, Part 1 Opens in Japan Today

[*Sound! Euphonium: The Final Movie – Part 1*](https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2026-02-21/sound-euphonium-the-final-movie-part-1-anime-film-main-trailer-highlights-new-scenes/.234404) opened across 200 Japanese theaters today, April 24, with a confirmed runtime of 120 minutes. Chief director Tatsuya Ishihara and director Taichi Ogawa lead the production, with TRUE performing the theme "ToCoda." The *Euphonium* franchise has been KyoAni's most sustained prestige project since 2015, and these finale films follow the same theatrical-recap format the studio used for earlier entries in the series. The gap between the TV third season and these films is frustrating for international audiences who want new material rather than a repackaged retelling — but KyoAni's theatrical versions have consistently improved on their source TV cuts in terms of visual quality, and the finale needs to close the story cleanly regardless of format. No international distribution window has been announced.

## Black Clover's Eleven-Year Run Ends April 30

Manga Plus [confirmed this week](https://x.com/AnimeSpotlightt/status/2046963505547575706) that *Black Clover* chapters 390, 391, and 392 will publish simultaneously on April 30 in the Spring 2026 issue of Jump GIGA. The issue includes a full-color lead page and interviews with both mangaka Yuki Tabata and voice actor Gakuto Kajiwara. [Chapters 387–389, published in January](https://comicbook.com/anime/feature/black-clover-ends-its-final-fight-and-a-winner-has-been-revealed/), concluded the battle against Lucius Zogratis; what's left on April 30 is denouement and epilogue. *Black Clover* debuted in 2015 and spent years as the title Shonen Jump published alongside *My Hero Academia* and *Demon Slayer* without ever matching their cultural footprint — which makes finishing on its own terms at volume 38 a meaningful outcome for a series that never had the easiest path. The ongoing anime Season 2 is still running, which means the manga ending will arrive before the animated version catches up.

## Gintama Creator's Debut Work *Dandelion* Is on Netflix — All Seven Episodes

All seven episodes of [*Dandelion*](https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2026-02-27/gintama-creator-hideaki-sorachi-dandelion-debut-1-shot-manga-gets-netflix-anime-series-in-april/.234651) are now available on Netflix, adapting the one-shot manga Hideaki Sorachi wrote before *Gintama*. The premise — two agents in a supernatural "Send-Off Department" guide restless spirits to the afterlife — is quieter and more melancholic than anything in *Gintama*'s catalog. Production is by NAZ, directed by Daisuke Mataga, with Yuki Hayashi composing and Chikahiro Kobayashi and Megumi Han as the leads. Netflix treated the launch with minimal press, consistent with how they handle smaller anime originals — the show arrived with a trailer and a premiere date and not much else. Seven episodes is a short window to establish a tone this different from Sorachi's established reputation; the whole series is available now, so whether it lands or doesn't is something you can find out in one sitting.

## Antiquarian Bookshop Biblia's Case Files Gets a CloverWorks TV Anime for 2027

[Kadokawa announced on April 21](https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2026-04-21/antiquarian-bookshop-biblia-mystery-novels-get-tv-anime-in-2027/.236656) that En Mikami's *Antiquarian Bookshop Biblia's Case Files* — a mystery novel series that has sold over 8.5 million copies across its 15-year run — will receive a TV anime adaptation from CloverWorks, set for 2027. Mamoru Kanbe directs; Toshiya Ono handles series composition; Jun Nakai designs the characters. Riko Akechi and Shunsuke Takeuchi are cast as the leads, Shioriko Shinokawa and Daisuke Gōra. The series already has a 2013 live-action drama adaptation, and the novels' core appeal — literary mysteries examined through rare book provenance — is the sort of property that anime has historically handled better in the contemplative mode than the high-action one. CloverWorks has the range to do this well, but their recent output has been uneven enough that the specific staff breakdown as it fills in matters more than the studio name alone.

## Cupid Parasite Is Getting a Steam Port — Idea Factory's First PC Otome in Eight Years

[Idea Factory International announced on April 22](https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2026-04-22/cupid-parasite-game-gets-pc-release/.236676) that *Cupid Parasite*, their 2021 Nintendo Switch otome visual novel, will release digitally on Steam in 2026, with a closed beta running April 27 through May 15. [Game8 notes](https://game8.co/articles/latest/cupid-parasites-steam-port-marks-idea-factorys-first-otome-pc-port-since-2018) this will be IFI's first PC otome port since 2018. The practical question is whether the otome audience — which has been console-first by habit and by circumstance — has migrated to Steam in meaningful numbers. Otome VNs have had a complicated history on the platform, partly due to Steam's content policies and partly due to the community skewing toward handheld play. If *Cupid Parasite*'s Steam numbers are strong, expect more of IFI's back catalog to follow.

## Diary of a Middle-Aged Sage Gets an Anime Adaptation

[Kadokawa announced on April 22](https://finalweapon.net/2026/04/22/the-diary-of-a-middle-aged-sages-carefree-life-in-another-world-light-novel-gets-anime-adaptation/) that Yasukiyo Kotobuki's light novel series *The Diary of a Middle-Aged Sage's Carefree Life in Another World* will be adapted as a TV anime at studio YANCHESTER, with Takayuki Inagaki directing and no broadcast window set. The premise — a middle-aged reincarnee who prioritizes comfort over heroics — is standard slow-life isekai, a subgenre that has demonstrated consistent commercial viability without producing many memorable entries. Nothing in the announcement distinguishes this one, and it doesn't need to: this lane has a reliable audience and a pipeline to match.

## Key's *Anemoi* Releases Today — First Original VN from the Studio Since 2020

Key (the studio behind *Clannad*, *Little Busters*, *Angel Beats*, and *Air*) released their new visual novel [*Anemoi*](https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2025-12-25/key-new-romantic-adventure-game-anemoi-delayed-to-april-24-2026/.232506) today on Steam with Japanese, English, and Simplified Chinese support, after a delay from its originally planned January 2026 date. The story follows protagonist Mugi Hayakawa returning with his sister to a rural northern Japanese town to unearth a time capsule buried ten years earlier, reconnecting with childhood friends along the way. The game ships at over 18GB across two physical discs — Key attributed the expanded footprint to enhanced visual direction, which is an unusual explanation for a VN release in an era where most studios are working leaner. *Summer Pockets: Reflection Blue* in 2020 was the last Key VN; *Anemoi* is their first fully new project since then, and the studio's parent company Visual Arts also [announced a new VN label this week](https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2026-04-21/visual-arts-company-unveils-new-visual-novel-brand-with-3-games/.236623), Visual Arts Scripts, with three new titles in development — suggesting the publisher has shifted from reissues back toward original work.

## Good Smile Company Announces Fire Emblem: Fates Azura Figures in Both Costume Variants

[Good Smile Company revealed](https://www.nintendolife.com/news/2026/04/good-smile-reveals-two-stunning-fire-emblem-figures-pre-orders-now-live) two 1/7-scale figures of Azura from *Fire Emblem Fates* — White Songstress and Dark Songstress variants — priced at $226.99 each, with pre-orders open through June 10 and an estimated delivery window of October–December 2027. *Fates* is over a decade old, but Azura remains a recurring figure subject because of her visual design rather than the game's narrative legacy. The dual-version strategy at this price point — $454 if you want both — is standard Good Smile practice for high-visibility characters: offer complete symmetry for the collector who wants the set, let individual buyers pick their preference. The eighteen-month wait from pre-order to delivery is also standard for this tier of figure production, and worth noting for anyone who treats these as impulse purchases.
