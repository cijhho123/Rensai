The experiment: take the Yuzusoft engine and run it without the fuel it was designed for.

[Yuzusoft](https://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Creator/Yuzusoft) has spent two decades building the same machine. [Sabbat of the Witch](https://store.steampowered.com/app/888790/Sabbat_of_the_witch/), [Senren\*Banka](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1101990/SenrenBanka/), [Riddle Joker](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1277930/Riddle_Joker/) — five or six heroine routes, exceptional character art, warm slice-of-life, a male lead who wants things and makes choices. The formula is reliable in the way that good craft is reliable: you know exactly what you're getting and it delivers. In 2021, they launched [Yuzusoft Sour](https://www.igdb.com/companies/yuzusoft-sour), a dedicated all-ages subsidiary, with [PARQUET](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1662840/PARQUET/) — a kinetic science fiction VN with no heroine routes, no adult content, and no choices. One path, ~15 hours, one ending.

The results are more instructive than the reviews let on.

![PARQUET key visual](https://animebackgrounds.home.blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/parquet-review-key-art.jpg)

## The Premise Earns Its Setup

PARQUET takes place in a near future where Brain-Machine Interface technology has enabled the digitization and transfer of human memories. The protagonist, Kanato, is an illegal experiment — thousands of memories pulled from thousands of different people, synthesized into a single consciousness. He has never had an original experience. Everything he knows, everything he feels, belongs to someone else. The central question — are we human? — is printed on the title screen and [threaded through every review](https://www.nookgaming.com/parquet-review/).

[The sci-fi setting](https://operationrainfall.com/2021/08/30/yuzusoft-visual-novel-parquet-now-available/) doesn't exist as window dressing. The BMI framework drives the story's argument about memory, identity, and the relationship between experience and selfhood. Kanato's condition forces the game to confront something eroge almost never has to address: what does it mean to desire something when the desire itself might be borrowed rather than real?

This is rich material. [The execution is uneven](https://noisypixel.net/parquet-review-pc/).

## The Heroines Are the Argument

Where PARQUET genuinely succeeds is in Kido Tsubasa and Ibaraki Rino — and the success is structural, not just characterization. Tsubasa is the Daytime Sunflower: exuberant, forward, working at a café, filling every room she enters. Rino is the Night Rose: working at a flower shop in the evening, quiet and guarded. They are, the game eventually reveals, the same person. One body, two selves — one existing only in daylight, one only after dark.

This is the game's best idea. And it is not a gimmick. Tsubasa and Rino's split-self condition mirrors Kanato's assembled-memory condition: both are *configurations* rather than unified persons, both products of fragmentation, one fractured by time, the other built from other people's pasts. The [Alice Glint figures announced for February 2026](https://solarisjapan.com/en-fr/blogs/news/parquet-alice-glint-tsubasa-rino) make this literal — interchangeable face plates and hair, one swapping seamlessly into the other, because physically they are the same person displayed in different modes.

![PARQUET Tsubasa and Rino](https://cdn.akamai.steamstatic.com/steam/apps/1662840/header.jpg)

What makes the heroines work is that their incompleteness is visible. The relationship between Tsubasa and Rino — two halves of one person who cannot share the same world — is [written with a maturity](https://www.nookgaming.com/parquet-review/) that Yuzusoft's five-route eroge titles rarely achieve, because those titles distribute the emotional weight across five separate arcs and none gets enough room. The single-path constraint forces the game to do something multi-route VNs almost never manage: treat the relationship between heroines as the story itself, rather than as a competition for the protagonist's attention.

## The Protagonist Problem, Or: What the Eroge Format Actually Does

Kanato fails in the same way every eroge-to-all-ages conversion fails when the developers don't recognize why the original format worked.

In Yuzusoft's eroge — in Riddle Joker, in Sabbat of the Witch — the male protagonist functions through desire. He has preferences. He makes choices. The route structure is the mechanism by which the reader projects romantic agency onto him, and through that agency the protagonist becomes real. He wants things, and what he wants is what the reader chooses him to want. This is not sophisticated characterization, but it works — because the reader's own investment fills the gaps.

PARQUET removes this mechanism. Kanato has no routes, makes no romantic choices, and is [deliberately written as passive](https://noisypixel.net/parquet-review-pc/) — the logic being that a being assembled from others' memories would have no authentic desires of his own. This is thematically coherent. Kanato's passivity is the argument. But the execution delivers [exactly the wrong result](https://www.nookgaming.com/parquet-review/): because he doesn't act on desire, doesn't impose himself on the story, and doesn't generate chemistry with either heroine, the reader has nothing to attach to. He becomes a camera rather than a character. His inner monologue — which should be the space where the "assembled self" theme lives and breathes — rarely offers the searching, uncertain interiority the premise demands.

[The Steam community noticed](https://steamcommunity.com/app/1662840/discussions/0/3051737278915385615/) what Yuzusoft Sour didn't anticipate: a Yuzusoft VN with one person voicing both heroines instead of a protagonist-as-romantic-lead left something structurally hollow. That hollow is Kanato. The heroines are complete enough on their own. The protagonist is a gap where a person should be.

## The Kinetic Form as Honest Admission

PARQUET's kinetic structure — no choices, one path — is either its most honest decision or its most cowardly one, and both readings hold.

The honest reading: Kanato cannot make choices, because a being without an authentic self cannot author authentic decisions. A choice menu would be a lie. The single path is the only structurally truthful way to tell this story. [Reviews praising the game](https://fuwanovel.moe/reviews/2021/09/03/parquet-review-are-we-human/) note that the Tsubasa-Rino relationship benefits enormously from this — because there is no competing route, no fork where you choose one over the other, the game is forced to treat their bond as the central emotional axis.

The cowardly reading: a kinetic novel is the safest possible format for a studio that has never made one. No choices means no wrong choices. No player agency means no way to fail to satisfy it. PARQUET sidesteps the hardest part of VN design — generating dramatically different character arcs from the same premise across multiple routes — and calls the absence a feature.

Both readings produce the same experience: [a 15-hour game](https://noisypixel.net/yuzusoft-parquet-english-pc-mobile/) that is genuinely moving in its second half, consistently beautiful to look at, and structurally more coherent than most five-route eroge — but with a protagonist-shaped hole at the center of every scene.

## What the Experiment Proves

[PARQUET holds a 95% positive rating on Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1662840/PARQUET/) from nearly 1,800 purchasers. The Yuzusoft craft survives the genre transplant: the art, the voice performances — headlined by [Touyama Nao](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nao_T%C5%8Dyama), known to any anime viewer for Chitoge in Nisekoi, Yui in OreGairu, Tomoe in Bunny Girl Senpai — the emotional intelligence in the heroine writing. [What doesn't survive](https://animebackgrounds.home.blog/2024/09/08/parquet/) is the protagonist function, because the eroge format was doing more for that function than anyone at Yuzusoft Sour seemed to recognize when they designed Kanato.

The lesson of PARQUET is not that Yuzusoft cannot do all-ages. It is that the eroge route structure, for all its repetitiveness, serves a real dramatic purpose: it gives the protagonist a stake. Without romantic stakes, without choice architecture, the male lead of a Yuzusoft VN is almost nothing. The heroines were always the real product. The routes were always the heroines' stories. PARQUET just removed the pretense.
