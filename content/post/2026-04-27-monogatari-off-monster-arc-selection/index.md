The first volume of the Off Season — *Orokamonogatari* — contains three short novels. The anime adapted one. The choice of which one is the most interesting creative decision in *Monogatari Series: Off & Monster Season*, and the two it skipped are at least as informative as the one it kept.

![Monogatari Series: Off & Monster Season key visual](https://static.animecorner.me/2024/03/1711264240-94259.jpg)

*Orokamonogatari*'s three arcs are Sodachi Fiasco, Suruga Bonehead, and Tsukihi Undo. Sodachi Oikura reappearing after her devastating Final Season arc. Suruga Kanbaru — probably the franchise's most beloved secondary character — continuing her story. And Tsukihi Araragi, the immortal phoenix, the "fake" little sister who has always existed at the edges of the main plot's attention. The anime [chose Tsukihi Undo](https://bakemonogatari.fandom.com/wiki/Monogatari_Series_Off_%26_Monster_Season). It didn't quietly skip the other two — the Off Season material simply isn't being adapted chronologically, which means the selection was made on purpose. That purpose is the article.

---

## What the Off Season Was Designed to Be

The Off Season novels were released from [2015 to 2017](https://bakemonogatari.fandom.com/wiki/Orokamonogatari), beginning with *Orokamonogatari* in October 2015. Structurally, they were a deliberate departure from everything that came before. Where the Final Season had built toward a climactic Araragi-centric resolution, the Off Season is [deliberately fragmented](https://bakemonogatari.fandom.com/wiki/Monogatari_Series_Off_%26_Monster_Season) — multiple narrators, self-contained stories, character-specific rather than chronological. As [the anime's official site frames it](https://monogatari-usa.com/oms/), the Off Season exists precisely because "the story of the girls who were saved by Araragi was not over" after his own arc concluded — the disunity of the novels reflects a universe that doesn't have a single center anymore.

The Monster Season (2017 onward) is [structurally tighter](https://bakemonogatari.fandom.com/wiki/Monogatari_Series_Off_%26_Monster_Season) — alternating between two primary narrative lines, more linear than Off Season's scattershot ensemble approach. It completes arcs the Off Season deliberately left open.

When production decisions were made for the anime, the team [deliberately interleaved](https://bakemonogatari.fandom.com/wiki/Monogatari_Series_Off_%26_Monster_Season) content from both seasons rather than adapting either in publication order. Producer Tatsuya Ishikawa told [Cocotame](https://cocotame.jp/en/series/101833/) that the revival began in 2022 around the Final Season Blu-ray BOX project, and the creative philosophy was "just do what you like." What they liked reveals a great deal about what they think this continuation is for.

The first cour's four arcs are: Tsukihi Undo (from *Orokamonogatari*, Off Season Book 1), Nadeko Draw (from *Nademonogatari*, Off Season Book 3), Acerola Bon Appétit (from *Wazamonogatari*, Off Season Book 2), and Shinobu Mustard (from *Shinobumonogatari*, Monster Season Book 1). They were adapted in that order — which is [not the publication order](https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/encyclopedia/anime.php?id=31635) of either season.

## The Characters Who Still Had Questions

What Tsukihi Undo, Nadeko Draw, Acerola Bon Appétit, and Shinobu Mustard have in common is that each centers on a character with unresolved selfhood. Not unresolved plot — unresolved identity.

Tsukihi Araragi is an immortal phoenix whose supernatural nature was revealed in *Nisemonogatari* but never examined from her own perspective. She won't age. She won't die. But each death fully replaces her with someone who shares her memories, her face, her relationships — the continuity of identity across those replacements is Tsukihi's specific existential question, and it's the one that the main series was too busy to ask properly. The arc is [narrated by Yotsugi Ononoki](https://wrongeverytime.com/2024/11/25/monogatari-off-monster-season-episode-1/), not Tsukihi herself — a deliberate choice that signals the ensemble shift. The premiere's extended 35-minute runtime (enabled by [ABEMA streaming removing traditional broadcast constraints](https://cocotame.jp/en/series/101833/)) invests that time in dialogue density: Yotsugi observing Tsukihi, Yotsugi discovering her own feelings about mortality in the process.

Nadeko Sengoku is the franchise's most unpopular character and its most interesting rehabilitation case. *Otorimonogatari*'s revelation that she had been performing passivity while harboring genuine rage made her divisive; the *Nademonogatari* arc [confronts her five past selves](https://medium.com/anitay-official/monogatari-off-season-nademonogatari-anime-review-doctorkev-and-lia-9ceb7309d102) — the five versions of Nadeko who were afraid, performative, violent, withdrawn, and searching — and asks her to integrate rather than reject them. The arc's argument, according to [the Beneath the Tangles analysis](https://beneaththetangles.com/2024/08/26/nadekos-benevolent-author/), is about the author figure as grace rather than judgment: who you were doesn't disqualify who you're becoming.

Shinobu Oshino (Kiss-Shot Acerola-Orion Heart-Under-Blade) is 600 years old and only recently learned she was capable of genuine connection. *Wazamonogatari*'s Acerola Bon Appétit arc reveals her original vampiric master — Deathtopia Virtuoso Suicide-Master — and in doing so, retroactively explains her 598 years of isolation. She couldn't bond because no one had taught her that bonding was possible. [The Shinobu Mustard arc](https://bakemonogatari.fandom.com/wiki/Monogatari_Series_Off_%26_Monster_Season) then shows what she and Araragi look like in a reduced, functional role — solving a new vampire problem without the weight of the original series' emotional architecture. These two arcs are the before and after: here is who she was before she could love, here is what that love looks like now.

## What the Omissions Say

The two omitted *Orokamonogatari* arcs — Sodachi Fiasco and Suruga Bonehead — share a quality the selected arc lacks: their characters had something like resolution in the main series.

Sodachi Oikura's Final Season arc was one of the most complete character studies in the entire franchise. Her hatred of Araragi, her isolation, her damaged relationship with her mother, the childhood misunderstanding that defined her adolescence — all of it was examined, named, and arrived at a kind of peace. What more does Sodachi Fiasco have to open? A version of her life post-resolution. That's not nothing, but it's different in kind from Tsukihi's unresolved phoenix identity.

Suruga Kanbaru is probably even clearer. The Rainy Devil arc was exactly what Kanbaru needed: confrontation with her own desires, including the violent ones. She processed her unrequited love for Senjougahara, her ambivalence about her own aggressive impulses, and her complicated inheritance. [Konshuu's analysis of her arc](https://issuu.com/konshuu-caa/docs/50-5/s/11282952) frames her lingering feelings as "a clear, unfulfillable love. One that forces you to move on." She did move on. Suruga Bonehead is presumably that moving on — which is valuable but not urgent. No question is eating at her the way phoenix continuity eats at Tsukihi.

*Musubimonogatari*, the fourth and closing Off Season volume, hasn't been reached in the first cour — and this is perhaps the most significant omission structurally. If closure were the production's goal, you'd lead with your concluding volume or at least orient toward it. The fact that the first cour builds toward the Monster Season rather than toward *Musubimonogatari* tells you the production is building an argument about unfinished characters, not delivering a graceful denouement to a finished franchise.

## NisiOisin's Unusually Direct Involvement

The adaptation is unusually faithful to its source material's priorities because NisiOisin [attends every script meeting and voice recording session](https://cocotame.jp/en/series/101833/). Hiroshi Kamiya, who voices Araragi, [compares scripts against the source material](https://cocotame.jp/en/series/101823/) and questions differences down to grammatical particles — a detail Ishikawa cited to illustrate the production's commitment to NisiOisin's exact phrasing. NisiOisin personally wrote the short story that became the basis for [YOASOBI's "UNDEAD" ending theme](https://cocotame.jp/en/series/101833/). The author's presence at the production table means the arc selection almost certainly reflects his thinking about which stories were ready to tell — and which ones his characters weren't finished with.

The [Sakuga Blog's analysis of director Midori Yoshizawa](https://blog.sakugabooru.com/2024/08/23/monogataris-past-present-and-future-midori-yoshizawa-oms/) notes she brings "a new era of visual creativity that synergizes with its themes of time, identity, and creation." Her 4:3 framing for past sequences, her use of actual food photography in the Shinobu arc, her tendency to design character spaces as manga panel layouts — these techniques are not decorative additions to a received visual style. They extend the franchise's established principle that form and content are inseparable. That the new director applies this principle to the specific thematic concerns of her selected arcs — selfhood, time, constructed identity — suggests the arc selection and the visual language were developed together.

## The Franchise That Didn't Want to Close

The second cour of Off & Monster Season was [announced for 2025](https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/.195680) with no specific release date. What remains unadapted: *Musubimonogatari*, and four Monster Season volumes — *Yoimonogatari*, *Amarimonogatari*, *Ougimonogatari*, *Shinomonogatari*. Some of those names — *Ougimonogatari*, a volume about Ougi — suggest the Monster Season contains precisely the kind of unresolved selfhood material that drove the first cour's selection logic.

*Monogatari*'s narrator has always been [unreliable as a structural matter](https://formeinfullbloom.wordpress.com/2017/02/17/more-on-bakemonogatari-and-narration/), filtering reality through subjective perception. Off & Monster Season extends this: the ensemble of narrators means no single subjective filter dominates. But the arc selection introduces a second layer of editorialization — the production team's own choices about what to tell, what to defer, and what to omit permanently.

The curated first cour doesn't end a franchise. It proposes a thesis: that the characters whose stories mattered most weren't the ones who got resolved in the main series — they were the ones who didn't. Tsukihi still doesn't know what continuity means for someone who dies and returns. Nadeko still had to face every version of herself she'd rather not remember. Shinobu had 598 years of incapacity to account for.

These aren't open threads that got left behind. They're the things the franchise was always building toward once Araragi got out of the way.
