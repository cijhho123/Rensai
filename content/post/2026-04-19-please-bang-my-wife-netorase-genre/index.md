The title requires a brief taxonomy lesson before anything else. *Netorare* (NTR) — the umbrella term Western audiences use for infidelity-based eroge — actually describes a specific emotional structure: the male protagonist loses his partner to another man, against his will, and the arousal is generated by his suffering. [*Netorase*](https://en.namu.wiki/w/%EB%84%A4%ED%86%A0%EB%9D%BC%EC%84%B8) is a different word describing a different thing: the husband (or boyfriend) not only accepts his partner's affair but actively facilitates it. He watches, arranges, requests. The arousal comes not from victimhood but from orchestration.

[*Please Bang My Wife*](https://operationrainfall.com/2016/10/21/18-eroge-review-please-bang-wife/) (Atelier Sakura, 2012; MangaGamer English localization September 2016) is a netorase game, despite routinely being shelved with NTR titles. This distinction is not pedantry — it changes what the game is actually doing, and what question it is trying to ask. Yasushi Sashihara, a struggling eroge writer married to a woman named Mariko, does not discover his wife's infidelity and then endure it helplessly. He suspects, confronts, extracts a confession through a sustained interrogation of Mariko that forms the structural core of the game, and then makes his request: he wants to watch.

The game's [interrogation mechanic](https://blog.mangagamer.org/2016/09/19/an-introduction-to-please-bang-my-wife/) — [documented in detail in player walkthroughs](https://www.otakulair.com/english-visual-novel-walkthroughs/please-bang-my-wife-walkthrough-mangagamer/) — is its most interesting formal choice. Mariko's encounters with Okada Kazuki (Yasushi's boss, the affair partner) are never shown directly to the player. They are recounted verbally by Mariko as Yasushi questions her, sometimes with false or evasive details that Yasushi challenges. This is either a principled decision — keeping the female body mediated through female testimony rather than direct male voyeurism — or an economical one for a short-form eroge. The effect, either way, is that Mariko's accounts carry their own texture and reliability. You are hearing her story, filtered through her.

## The Problem the Wife Route Introduces

*Please Bang My Wife* includes an unlockable route from [Mariko's perspective](https://operationrainfall.com/2016/10/21/18-eroge-review-please-bang-wife/). This is where the game's genre positioning gets complicated.

In the netorase structure, the husband's desire is primary. He is the one who wants this. The wife's participation is, in the cleaner versions of the genre, framed as responsive — she has the affair because he wants her to, because the arrangement has his blessing, because his desire has created the permission structure. The husband is the architect; the wife is the material. Her pleasure is real, but it is in service of his fantasy.

Mariko's route disrupts this. Through her perspective, the player learns that [her relationship with Okada predated Yasushi's arrangement and his blessing](https://videogamegeek.com/videogame/270916/please-bang-my-wife). She was not set up to have an affair by a husband who wanted to be cuckolded. She was already having one, for reasons that belong to her. The [Operation Rainfall review identifies this](https://operationrainfall.com/2016/10/21/18-eroge-review-please-bang-wife/) as suggesting Mariko is "genuinely poly-amorous" — not a cheater in the conventional sense, not simply an unfaithful wife, but a person who needs more than one romantic connection and is coming to terms with that need.

This is a meaningful claim. If it is true — if Mariko's desire for Okada is autonomous, pre-existing, and not a product of Yasushi's fantasy — then the game has introduced a female subject with genuine interiority into a genre that typically treats women as objects through which the male protagonist experiences himself. The wife is not the instrument of the husband's netorase. The husband's netorase is an accommodation of the wife's already-existing desire.

## What the Genre Does With This

The netorase genre, as [TV Tropes and fan documentation describe it](https://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Main/Netorare), typically positions female desire as passive or corrupted — the woman "gives in" to a stronger man because she has been persuaded or seduced away from her primary relationship. Her agency, if it appears at all, is framed as weakness. [The broader netorare genre is routinely criticized for exactly this](https://allthetropes.org/wiki/Netorare_Genre): its tendency to portray women as easily redirected toward whoever provides better sex, their personalities irrelevant to the genre machinery.

Netorase complicates this by repositioning the husband from victim to conspirator — but it does not necessarily change the status of the wife. She still has sex with another man. The husband still derives arousal from this. Her desires remain subordinate to the structure he has authorized. The genre's women-as-objects problem does not disappear; it gets a new frame.

What's interesting about *Please Bang My Wife*'s wife route is that it gestures toward something else. If Mariko's polyamory is genuine and prior to Yasushi's arrangement, then she is not a participant in his netorase fantasy — she is a person whose actual desires happen to overlap with it. The fantasy is secondhand. Yasushi is accommodating her, not directing her.

The game does not fully commit to this reading. It is a short eroge from a [studio known for accessible, affect-forward NTR production](https://www.giantbomb.com/atelier-sakura/3010-13284/) — [Atelier Sakura is credited with contributing to the "popularization of the NTR genre"](https://x.com/ErogeAreAlive/status/1882707490481250432), and their catalog, [now closed after a January 2025 shutdown](https://www.4gamers.com.tw/news/detail/69818/atelier-sakura-closure-announcement) marking 15 years of operation, prioritized genre delivery over literary ambition. The wife route is unlockable bonus content, not the primary narrative engine. Asking it to carry the weight of a serious exploration of female desire is probably unfair to the work's intentions.

## What the Interrogation Structure Actually Does

The more durable formal argument is about the interrogation scenes. [The MangaGamer introduction notes](https://blog.mangagamer.org/2016/09/19/an-introduction-to-please-bang-my-wife/) that "much of the story revolves around Yasushi's suffering" as he extracts Mariko's confession — and that Mariko "sometimes provides false details that Yasushi must challenge." This creates a dynamic where Mariko is not simply narrating but negotiating. She has information Yasushi wants. She has reasons to withhold or misrepresent it. The game's erotic tension emerges from the process of getting her to tell the truth.

This is a curious structure. In a pure netorare game, the wife's testimony would be presented as evidence of her corruption — her account of pleasure with another man would be framed as betrayal. Here, her testimony is something Yasushi wants, requests, extracts. Her pleasure with Okada is not incidental to his desire; it is the substance of it. He is not a victim listening to a confession; he is a confessor drawing it out.

[The NamuWiki documentation of netorase](https://en.namu.wiki/w/%EB%84%A4%ED%86%A0%EB%9D%BC%EC%84%B8) notes that the genre's appeal lies in consent removing the "guilt that comes from netorare" while preserving the "feelings of immorality." *Please Bang My Wife* operates squarely in this space: Yasushi wants to feel the immorality without the involuntary suffering. The interrogation format delivers exactly this — he controls the information, therefore controls his experience of it.

This is also why the wife's genuine polyamory, if taken seriously, is so destabilizing for the fantasy. Yasushi can only be the orchestrator of something that wasn't already in motion. If Mariko's desire for Okada is real and autonomous, Yasushi was not directing the performance — he was just given a seat.

## Atelier Sakura's Legacy in a Closed Genre

[Atelier Sakura's shutdown](https://www.4gamers.com.tw/news/detail/69818/atelier-sakura-closure-announcement) — 15 years after founding, announced January 2025 — marks the end of one of the more productive studios in the NTR/netorase subgenre. Their catalog, which [included *Remember* and *Kashidashi Tsuma*](https://www.giantbomb.com/atelier-sakura/3010-13284/) alongside *Please Bang My Wife*, defined a specific style of affect-forward marital infidelity eroge: short, focused, cheap, and deliberately aimed at a fandom that knew exactly what it was buying.

*Please Bang My Wife* is representative of this approach. It is not trying to be a serious examination of polyamory, or a literary investigation of jealousy, or a feminist reclamation of female desire. It is trying to deliver a specific genre experience efficiently. That it accidentally introduces a formal tension — the wife's perspective suggesting she has desires that predate and exceed the husband's fantasy — is either the game being smarter than it appears, or the genre's structural logic creating complications it didn't intend.

Either way, the title is more interesting than it looks. Whose fantasy is it? Yasushi's, technically — he made the request. Mariko's, by her own account — she was already there.
