There is a scene early in the series where Lakan first makes himself known to Maomao as a child. He has just been beaten. His face is bloodied and swollen. He appears in front of her and reaches toward her and says, plainly, that he is her father.

She sees a terrifying monster. [She despises the very sight of him from that moment forward](https://kusuriya.fandom.com/wiki/Maomao/History).

What she doesn't know — what neither she nor the audience knows for a long time — is that the bloodied monster looking at her is experiencing the scene entirely differently. For Lakan, this is the second time in his entire life he has stood in front of a person who has a face.

## What Prosopagnosia Actually Does

[Prosopagnosia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prosopagnosia) — face blindness — is a real neurological condition affecting approximately one in thirty-three people, though the severity varies enormously. In its most profound form, the affected person can see individual facial features perfectly well but cannot integrate them into recognizable identity. [Every face is a collection of parts that don't resolve into a person](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/diseases/23412-prosopagnosia-face-blindness). Familiar people and strangers are indistinguishable by sight. Some with the condition cannot recognize their own face in a mirror.

[Research notes that prosopagnosia involves a specific disconnection](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK559324/): not a loss of knowledge about people, but an inability to access identity through facial features. You can know perfectly well who someone is. You just can't see it. The condition is not curable. The adaptations people develop involve voice recognition, gait, distinctive features (a scar, unusual hair), and context — the kinds of things that work in controlled environments and fail completely in unfamiliar settings.

[Lakan has had this from birth](https://kusuriya.fandom.com/wiki/Lakan). His uncle Luomen taught him to navigate it by associating people with game pieces — strangers as Go stones (black for men, white for women), known people as shogi pieces ranked according to their significance in his life. This coping mechanism, adopted out of practical necessity, [made him one of the greatest military tacticians in the empire](https://kusuriya.fandom.com/wiki/Lakan/History). He reads the world as a strategy board because the world literally appears to him as one.

And then he walked into the Verdigris House and met a courtesan named Fengxian who could play Go and shogi at his level. And [she was the first person who appeared to him with a face](https://kusuriya.fandom.com/wiki/Lakan/History).

## The Architecture of the Exception

The series' explanation for why Lakan can see Fengxian and Maomao is not entirely scientific — the narrative frames it as emotional attachment allowing the perceptual block to dissolve. The [scientific literature on prosopagnosia](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5398751/) doesn't quite support this; the condition doesn't typically resolve through emotional intensity. But within the story's internal logic, this is the most precise possible statement: Lakan can see as complete human beings only the people he loves most completely.

![The Apothecary Diaries light novel volume 1 cover](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/e/e1/The_Apothecary_Diaries_volume_1.jpg)

What this means is devastating. Fengxian — the first real face — was also the woman whose life he ruined. He left for a military campaign before she knew she was pregnant. [His family intercepted her letters for three years](https://kusuriya.fandom.com/wiki/Lakan/History). She bore Maomao alone, lost her status as a high-ranking courtesan, became a streetwalker, contracted syphilis. By the time Lakan returned and sought her out, she was barely recognizable physically — and yet, in an act of terrible grace, he still saw the same face he had memorized years before. The illness could not change her to him because what he had memorized was a resolution of her identity, not a photograph. 

Maomao — the second real face — is the product of that relationship. She did not choose her father. She experienced him as a monster, then as a threat, then as an embarrassment. She has spent her entire story avoiding him. And from inside the condition that defines how he perceives reality, she is one of only two people in the world who have existed to him as a person rather than a piece.

## The Cruelty of the Irony

The series earns this structural irony because it shows both sides with equal clarity. [From Maomao's perspective](https://kusuriya.fandom.com/wiki/Maomao), and therefore from ours for most of the narrative, Lakan is objectively difficult to defend: intrusive, oblivious, apparently indifferent to the destruction his absence and his family's machinations caused. [Fan response shifted significantly](https://www.cbr.com/the-apothecary-diaries-lakan-maomao-relationship/) once his backstory was revealed — not because the harm was undone, but because the harm was seen from both sides for the first time.

The mechanism of "fans did a complete 180 on their perception of Lakan" is itself worth examining. Nothing Lakan did changes when the backstory is revealed. Fengxian still went insane. Maomao was still raised without her father. The letters still went unopened. What changes is the same thing that changes in [the Ougi Formula arc of Owarimonogatari](https://bakemonogatari.fandom.com/wiki/Owarimonogatari_Episode_01_%26_02:_Ougi_Formula) — the realization that the most important information was always there, just not accessible from the position the narrative had given you. The architecture of the story decides what you can see. When the architecture shifts, the person you were judging becomes someone else — not because they changed, but because you now have a different vantage point. [FandomWire noted how the community's response shifted](https://fandomwire.com/lakans-rare-disorder-in-the-apothecary-diaries-made-season-1s-ending-even-more-romantic-than-fans-realise/) once the condition was explained in full — suddenly moments that had seemed creepy read differently, including the Season 1 finale where Lakan sits with Fengxian and sees the face he has always seen, unchanged by illness.

This is also what the series does structurally: it is built around a protagonist whose tools are pharmaceutical knowledge and medical reasoning, and whose central limitation is that knowledge doesn't automatically produce compassion. [Maomao's expertise comes from women's spaces](https://kusuriya.fandom.com/wiki/Maomao) — from Luomen's practice, from the courtesans of the Verdigris House — but her emotional architecture is organized around protection and distance. She deflects Jinshi's attempts at warmth with professional deflection, refuses to acknowledge emotional debts she didn't ask to incur, and processes threats by solving them rather than feeling them. She does not easily extend the same analytical attention to people she has categorized as threats or burdens — [and the series is honest that this is what Lakan is to her](https://fandomwire.com/the-apothecary-diaries-we-dont-realise-just-how-helpless-of-a-father-lakan-has-been-to-maomao/) for most of the narrative.

![Lakan character art from The Apothecary Diaries fandom wiki](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/kusuriya/images/4/4c/Lakan.png/revision/latest?cb=20231217022200)

When she finally sits across from Lakan at a Go board and begins to actually look at him — not as the monster from her childhood or the imposition of her adult life, but as a player — she is doing what Lakan has done her entire life: perceiving him through the lens of the game. The [episode 23 reveal](https://www.comingsoon.net/anime/news/1607451-the-apothecary-diaries-episode-23-recap-spoilers-maomao-challenges-lakan-to-a-chess-game) is built around this moment of mirrored perception: two people seeing each other finally through a shared framework, even if neither would name it as such.

## What It Costs

The tragedy of Lakan's condition in this story is not that it makes him a bad father. It's that it makes him a father who could not be recognized as one. His love for Maomao is, within the logic of his condition, the most complete form of love he can experience — she has a face to him. She is real to him in a way that almost no one else is. And she experiences this as predatory attention, as an unwanted claim, as the gaze of the man who abandoned her mother.

The [series wisely refuses to resolve this fully](https://www.cbr.com/the-apothecary-diaries-lakan-maomao-relationship/). Knowing about Lakan's prosopagnosia doesn't retroactively make him a good father or give Maomao back the childhood she didn't have. What it does is complicate the moral universe the story operates in — which is exactly what good backstory does. The two faces Lakan can see are the two women whose lives his choices shaped most catastrophically. His condition doesn't excuse anything. It just means that the people he has most affected are also the only people who, for him, have ever truly been there.

That's not poetic. That's structural. And it's why Lakan is one of the better-written characters in recent historical anime, despite — or because of — how uncomfortable he makes everyone for most of the story.
