2026-04-26: deconstruction - Argued that Isaki's insomnia is mortality anxiety (fear her heart will stop in the night), that the heartbeat-as-cure mechanism is the series' genuine romantic core, and that Ojiro's refusal to use the illness for tragedy is its most significant artistic choice — rebutting the 'boring' criticism as an audience expectation problem.
2026-04-27: cultural-impact - Argued that the series' obsessive location specificity — real railways, real archaeological sites, real dark skies — retroactively transformed Insomniacs After School into an inadvertent archive when the 2024 Noto earthquake struck and damaged the places it had spent four years documenting.
2026-04-29: character-analysis - Argued that Nakami Ganta's abandonment-rooted insomnia (mother left in the night without warning) is the series' sharpest psychological detail, structurally opposite to Isaki's mortality anxiety, and that his arc through astrophotography and Isaki's presence constitutes the manga's real argument about how adolescent wounds heal — through replacement experience, not treatment.
