2026-04-27: character-analysis - Argued that Rebellion's controversial ending — Homura as Devil usurping Madoka — is more faithful to Homura's character than Urobuchi's planned ending (graceful ascension), because Homura has always refused every exit that didn't involve Madoka being present and reachable; the commercial compromise that produced the ending turned out to be more psychologically honest than the author's intent.
2026-04-29: thematic-analysis/deconstruction - Argued that Rebellion's false paradise is not the girls' paradise but Homura's self-portrait: each gift is bounded by what she knows and projects about each girl — Madoka gets the ordinary life she consciously chose to sacrifice; Mami gets Charlotte (drawn from fan desire absorbed by the production staff, not from Mami's own wishes); Sayaka gets heroism and Kyoko but not Kyosuke; Nagisa gets 'normal childhood' as an imagined category. The false world is Homura's thesis about happiness, and a thesis is not the same as understanding.
